# Political image and image–text coding guide

## Visual content

Record the observable scene: portrait, small group, crowd, street activity, meeting, speech, building/place, flag/banner/symbol, screenshot/document, map/chart, merchandise or other. Avoid identifying people unless the article itself explicitly names them and the research question requires it.

## Political visual functions

- **Identity and belonging:** visual construction of a collective or place.
- **Mobilisation:** invitation, participation, action or event documentation.
- **Legitimation:** authority, institutional setting, expertise, tradition or historical continuity.
- **Evidence/documentation:** image presented as showing that an event or claim occurred.
- **Emotionalisation:** imagery designed or functioning to evoke fear, pride, sympathy, anger or hope; code cautiously and explain observable cues.
- **Attack/ridicule:** delegitimising representation of an opponent or institution.
- **Commemoration:** remembrance, heritage or historical continuity.
- **Branding:** repeated colours, emblems, logos, banners or designed templates.
- **Decorative/unclear:** no defensible discursive function established.

## Relation to article text

Code **redundant**, **elaborative**, **complementary**, **evidential**, **contrasting**, **decorative**, or **unclear**. A low embedding similarity is only a sampling cue. It does not prove contradiction, manipulation or falsehood.

Do not perform face recognition, person re-identification, ethnicity/religion inference, emotion recognition from faces, or political-affiliation inference from appearance.
