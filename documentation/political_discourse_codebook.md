# Political-discourse coding guide

Use the automatic output only to retrieve candidate passages. Code the full sentence and, where necessary, its paragraph and article context.

## Textual categories

- **Nomination and categorisation:** how an actor is named, grouped or functionalised.
- **Predication:** qualities, actions or evaluations attributed to an actor.
- **Activation/passivation:** whether an actor appears as an agent, affected participant or backgrounded entity.
- **In-group/out-group construction:** explicit or implicit contrasts; do not assume every *we/they* token has this function.
- **Frame:** security/threat, economy/cost, culture/identity, law/order, democracy/representation, welfare/services, family/children, institutions/media, mobilisation, or an inductively derived frame.
- **Legitimation:** authorisation, moral evaluation, rationalisation or narrative legitimation.
- **Stance and evidentiality:** certainty, hedging, attribution, reported speech and source of a claim.
- **Presupposition/implicature:** information treated as given or communicated indirectly.
- **Strategic ambiguity/coded language:** record a literal reading, proposed contextual function and at least one alternative interpretation.

Always mark quotation and reported speech so that language attributed to another speaker is not automatically attributed to the publisher. Networks are textual co-occurrence networks, not evidence of organisational relationships.
